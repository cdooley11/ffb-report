🏈 Fantasy Football Franchise Player Transition

New Format Starting 2026

2026Transition Year

  • Keep 2 keepers at bottom of draft
  • Keep 1 keeper at previous year draft pick + 1 round (closest pick available)
Example: Draft in 6th round → Keep in 5th round next year. No 5th round pick? Use 4th round.

2027+Franchise Player Format

  • Keep 1 player at bottom of the draft
  • Keep 2 keepers at n+1 round available
Example: Your two keepers advance one round earlier each year (6th → 5th → 4th), while your franchise player is always kept at the bottom of the draft.

📋 Additional Rules to Be Aware of Pre-Draft

First Round Pick Restriction

1st round picks cannot be kept. However, 2nd round picks can be kept in the 1st round the following year, but will not qualify for a keeper after that.

Exception for 2026: If you want to keep all 3 players from last year (2025), you can keep 2 at bottom and 1 with a first round pick (counts as your keeper in the draft), you must tell commish who is the first round pick as they will be released to the draft in 2027.
Exception for 2027: One player from the bottom-of-draft keepers in 2026 may be kept as a 1st round pick in 2027 only (counts as one of your keepers in the draft), then must be released to the draft in 2028.

Traded Draft Picks

If you do not have a 1st round pick (traded away), you cannot keep that player who qualifies for that round of keeper (previous year kept in 2nd round or previous year drafted in 2nd round).

Multi-Year Keeper Progression

If you keep a player in the 5th round, you can keep them the following year at 4th round, then the following year in 3rd round, and so on.

Example: Draft in 6th → Keep in 5th → Keep in 4th → Keep in 3rd (and so on)

Missing Draft Pick Scenario

If you draft someone in the 6th round and do not have a 5th round pick the following year, it would be a 4th round keeper.

Free Agent Pickups

Free agent pickups will be kept in the 7th round the following year.

Example: If you want to keep a free agent and a player drafted in 8th round, it will use your 7th round pick & 6th round pick to keep both of those players.

Traded Players

If you trade for a player drafted in round X, you will keep them the following year in round X+1.

Example: I trade for a player who was drafted in the 5th round. The following year I keep them in the 4th round (or next available round if 4th round pick is not owned).

Drop and Re-Add (Same Team)

If you draft a player in the 5th round, drop them, and pick them back up, you do NOT keep them in the 7th round. You keep them in the 4th round the following year (still follows n+1 rule).

Same rule applies if you drop a 10th round pick and pick him back up. He is a 9th round keeper the following year.

Drop and Pick Up (Different Team)

If a player was drafted in the 3rd round and dropped, then picked up by another team, they are now a 7th round keeper the following year (free agent rule applies).

Optional Keepers

You are NOT required to keep any players if you do not have good value in the draft.

Stay informed. Know your keepers. Dominate your league.
